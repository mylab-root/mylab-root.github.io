Understanding the mechanism modulating ROS distributions under abiotic stress by live-imaging analysis

Exposure to various abiotic stresses results in accumulation of reactive oxygen species (ROS) in plants. Toxic levels of abiotic stress produce large amounts of ROS in specific cells or tissues. High ROS levels limit plant development. However, in moderate abiotic stress conditions, ROS has a signaling role in the regulation of plant development for adaptation. We will:

  • Visualize ROS signal using redox biosensor by live-image analysis and characterize the root system architecture of rice root by knockdown of target transcripts;
  • Investigate the mechanism resulting in the changes of ROS distributions among developmental zones under abiotic stress;
  • Knockdown the targets and detect ROS signals in the developmental zones under abiotic stress conditions by using redox sensitive biosensor in Arabidopsis root (Fig. 2);
  • Learn which transcript controls ROS distribution in the developmental zones by live-imaging analysis.
